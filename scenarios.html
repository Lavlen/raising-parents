How Would You Handle These Common Scenarios?

Scenario 1 of 10
1. Your neighbor or friend pulls you aside and says that they believe that your son is involved in a gang. You have never heard anything more absurd. Do you?
A. Say thank you and ignore the silly suggestion
B. Search your son’s room for evidence of this
C. Ask him, and accept what ever he says, trust is key
D. Look for evidence but ask him about it as well
Show Answer

A. Look for evidence discretely but give your child the opportunity to tell you if there is something going on that you need to know about.

Scenario 2 of 10
2. Almost overnight your 16 year old daughters behaviour changes. She no longer wants to spend time with the family and spends 95% of her time in her room. The way she dresses has changed and she seems to have lost contact with her two best friends. Do you?
A. Put it down to hormones, she going through lots of changes and this must be an effects of this. 
B. Insist she talks to you about it, it could be a sign of something deeper
C. Have a word with her friends to see if they could shed any light on the matter
D. Call her teacher to see if there has been anything going on at school
Show Answer

B. Insist she talks to you about it, it could be a sign of something deeper

Scenario 3 of 10
3. Your 8 year old suddenly demands a magazine that is not appropriate for her age. All her friends’ parents have bought it for their children, but you believe it to be age inappropriate. Your out-grocery shopping when she picks up the latest copy and demands that you purchase it, or else she’ll make a scene. She is notorious for her major meltdowns! Do you?
A. Get the magazine, to avoid the scene, the discuss it with her when you get home
B. Say no and deal with what ever outburst she displays when, or if it happens
C. Threaten to make a bigger scene!
D. Explain why this behaviour is unacceptable, then make the purchase
Show Answer

B. Say no and deal with what ever outburst she displays when, or if it happens. This should be followed up by a conversation explaining why this behaviour is unacceptable

Scenario 4 of 10
4. Whilst snooping… you find some porn magazines in your 12-year-old sons’ room, you know it will cripple him with embarrassment if you talk to him about it, do you?
A. Make him bear the embarrassment and challenge him about them
B. Put them back, maybe he’ll never notice. That way you can continue to monitor
C. Ask someone else to have a word
D. Gently approach the subject using TV or other talking points
Show Answer

D. Gently approach the subject using TV or other talking points

Scenario 5 of 10
5. Your 3-year-old child is getting frustrated as he tries to open a tight-fitting bottle, you attempt to help him. His responds by physically pushing you away and telling you to leave him alone. Do you?
A. Leave him to open the bottle, and not say anything it will be a good challenge
B. Take the bottle away and explain to him why, he should address you differently
C. Open the bottle whilst giving him a ‘talking to’
D. Let him attempt to open the bottle, when he fails, use this as an opportunity to explain why this is not acceptable. When he understands and apologizes open it
Show Answer

D. Let him attempt to open the bottle, when he fails, use this as an opportunity to explain why this is not acceptable. When he understands and apologizes open it

Scenario 6 of 10
6. Your 14-year-old son asks if he can sleep at his 14-year-old girlfriends house, her parents will be in for the whole night, do you?
A. Absolutely no way!
B. Let him stay but ask if its ok with her parents, after all they’ll keep an eye on them
C. Give him a thorough talking to about the birds and the bees
D. What’s the problem they're young, plus I trust my son
Show Answer

A. Absolutely no way! But explain to him that he's too young to be having sleepovers with girlfriends, plenty of time for that in the years to come

Scenario 7 of 10
7. You ask your teen to do their household chores which consist of washing up, tidying their room and putting the washing into the basket, they do everything with the exception of tidying their room. You challenge them and they say they’ll do it tomorrow. This lasts for 3 days. Do you?
A. Punish them for not doing their chores (no pocket money, no tv, etc)
B. Thank your child for what they have already done then explain to them That you would like it all done by a specific time.
C. Do it for them, after all they did the other two chores
D. Ask them again and again until they get it done
Show Answer

B. If your request is not unreasonable, explain to your child what your general expectations are when you ask them to do chores. Be willing to see through consequences you warned them about

Scenario 8 of 10
8. Should you pay your children as an incentive to do household chores?
A. There is nothing wrong with paying your child to do chores
B. If you can afford to pay them then why not?
C. Yes, it teaches them that money is earned through work
D. As a general rule, parents shouldnt be paying their children to do chores
Show Answer

D.

Scenario 9 of 10
9. Your 15-year-old has a curfew which they break by a couple of hours, do you?
A. Ground him, he should have come back on time
B. listen to his explanation, if its not good enough give a talk on being responsible before telling him how long he is not allowed to go out for
C. Give him a talk about responsibility, for the umpteenth time
D. Reduce his curfew time for next time, as a consequence of his actions.
Show Answer

B. Teach teens to take responsibility for their actions. Ensure they understand that their are consequences for actions or decisions we make in life.

Scenario 10 of 10
8. You’re at the park with your 4-year-old daughter when you see another child kick her, the parent of the child sees also but does nothing, do you?
A. Wipe her tears, give her a pep talk and send her off again, kids will be kids.
B. Advise her to kick back if it happens again.
C. Discuss it with the parent, who is sat on the bench next to you.
D. Move her to play with other kids.
Show Answer

C. Discuss it with the parent, who is sat on the bench next to you.
