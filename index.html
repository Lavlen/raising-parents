Are Our Kids Using Half their Brains!

Author: Marcia Brackenridge July|21, 2021

In early childhood (nursery / preschool) there appears to be an appreciation for development of both sides of the brain. Activities such as drawing, finger painting, singing, role play, play dough and number songs, writing and circle time are a few activities which focus on this development; very successfully might I add. However when children begin to grow and develop through their school careers the emphasis on developing both sides of the brain seem to diminish and greater emphasis is placed on the left side and are reinforced by way of tests such as CAT’s, SAT’s, GCSE.s etc...

Read more..

Whilst children are still encouraged to participate in dance, music, drama and other creative activities; this tends to be much more of an option than science, maths or English (which are essential). If the decline in activities which develop the right side of your child’s brain begins soon after children start primary school, what can we as parents do to continue this essential development, it seems obvious doesn't it! provide a replacement to fill this void? If we sent our children to school and suddenly the school stopped feeding them at lunchtimes, what would we do (apart from find a new school!) That’s right we'd supply a packed lunch for the child or make some alternative provision to ensure they get what they need.

Now for the practical bit...
